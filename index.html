Call Script
Follow each step in order. You are your name from the Business Loan Division.
Step 1Opening — Confirm Owner

"Hi, this is your name from the Business Loan Division. Am I speaking with the business owner?"

Yes / Owner on line
Proceed to Step 2 — deliver the pre-qual opener.
Not the owner
"Can I please speak with the owner or the managing partner — I'll hold."

If asked who's calling: "Again I'm your name, calling in regard to a personal business matter."
Step 2Pre-Qual Opener

If owner was already on the line:

"Perfect, I'll be quick. We're following up because you were prequalified for working capital or a business line of credit. I'm not calling to pull your credit or anything — we just ask a few questions to make sure your business is qualified for this."

If owner was transferred:

"Hi, this is your name from the Business Loan Division. We're following up because you were prequalified for working capital or a business line of credit. I'm not calling to pull your credit or anything — we just ask a few questions to make sure your business is qualified."

Step 3Qualification Questions
1
"Have you been in business for at least 1 year?"
MUST BE 1 YEAR+ — if no, does not qualify.
2
"What type of industry are you in?"
❌ No Attorneys · Lawyers · Banking · Lending · Mortgage · Church · Non-Profit
3
"Are you 100% owner, or do you have partners? If partners — do you own at least 50%?"
Must own at least 50%.
If less than 50%: "Is there a time we can get the majority partner on the line?" — set a callback.
4
"Does your business average at least $20,000 per month or more in deposits?"
MUST BE YES — nothing under $240K/year.

If ANY disqualifying answer is given — politely end the call. They do not qualify at this time.

Step 4Qualified — Warm Handoff

"Great! It looks like you meet the pre-qualification criteria. I'm going to connect you with a senior advisor who will go over your funding options. Just hang on one moment — I'm going to stay on the line with you during the handoff. I'll introduce you first when they answer."

Objection Handling
Rebuttals
Tap any objection to see the exact response. Stay calm, stay warm, keep moving.
Timing"I'm not interested."
"I completely understand — and I appreciate your honesty. I'm not here to sell you anything today. We're just verifying a few details because your business was flagged as pre-qualified. It literally takes 60 seconds and there's zero obligation. Can I ask just one quick question before I let you go?"
Keep your tone light and curious — not pushy. Frame it as confirming something they already have.
Skepticism"Is this a scam?" / "How did you get my number?"
"That's a completely fair question, and I respect you for asking. We're ATLeads, Business Loan Division. Your business information was submitted through a funding inquiry which is how you came up as pre-qualified. I'm not pulling any credit or asking for sensitive information — just a few qualifying questions. We work with real businesses every day."
Be confident, not defensive. Validate their concern and immediately re-anchor to the pre-qual angle.
Timing"I'm busy right now."
"No problem at all — I only need 60 seconds. This is specifically about funding that's already been set aside for your business. If now truly isn't good, when is a better time — later today or tomorrow morning?"
Always offer a specific callback time — don't leave it open-ended. Get a commitment.
Financial"I don't need a loan / I have enough money."
"That's actually the best time to look at working capital — when you don't need it. Smart business owners use funding to scale, hire, or take on bigger contracts without touching their own cash. It costs nothing to hear the options and you're under no obligation whatsoever."
Reframe — funding isn't a sign of need, it's a tool for growth.
Credit"I have bad credit."
"That's not necessarily a disqualifier. These are business-based funding options — we're looking at your business revenue and deposits, not just personal credit. A lot of business owners in your position have still been approved. Let me ask you a couple of quick questions to see where you stand."
Immediately transition to qualifying questions — keep momentum.
Already Have"I already have a loan / working with someone else."
"That's great that you're already set up — we're not here to replace anyone. What we offer is working capital on top of existing funding, and many business owners use multiple sources. It's worth a quick conversation to see if there's something additional available. It's completely free to check."
Don't compete — position it as additive, not a replacement.
Gatekeeper"Send me an email / Send information."
"Absolutely, I can do that. But just so I can send you the right information specific to your business, let me ask you just two quick questions first — it'll take less than a minute and I'll make sure whatever I send is actually relevant to what you qualify for."
Use email as a bridge to qualify first. Get Q1 and Q4 answered before agreeing to send anything.
Revenue"We don't make $20K a month."
"I appreciate you being upfront — I want to make sure I'm only connecting you with options you actually qualify for. Unfortunately the minimum threshold is $20,000 in monthly deposits. That said, keep building — once you're there, you'll be a great fit and I'd love to reconnect."
End gracefully. Don't push past a hard disqualifier.
Decision"I need to talk to my partner / spouse first."
"That makes total sense — major decisions are a team effort. Is there a time I can get both of you on the line together? Or I can call back at whatever time works best. I just want to make sure you have all the info when you have that conversation."
Set a specific callback with both decision-makers on the line.
